I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Clemson University, advised by Dr. Zhenkai Zhang. My research interests include computer systems security, side-channel attacks, and GPU security.

My current research focuses on practical security analysis of computing systems, including side-channel leakage, GPU behavior, cloud and shared-resource environments, and hardware-assisted measurement. I am interested in understanding how low-level system behavior can reveal security-relevant information and how such leakage can be measured, analyzed, and mitigated.

Before joining Clemson, I received my M.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Shahid Beheshti University and my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Semnan University.

My work has been accepted at IEEE HOST 2026. I am also a recipient of the Talford Family Endowed Fellowship in Computing at Clemson University.

This website includes information about my research, publications, teaching experience, and academic activities.
